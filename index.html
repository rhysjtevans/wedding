How we met

Jo and I met through a dating app. After a couple of weeks of chatting & getting to know each other I built up the courage and asked her out for a drink. Little did I know at the time she was putting me through a very rigorous and careful screening process!
Some how I made it through and she agreed to meet up for a drink.
We met up for the first time on the 15th December 2011 in the Tair Pluen in the heart of Cardiff City Centre. I was very nervous, I always remember her hair was a dark red, still in work clothes and like today, looked amazing and was very easy to talk to.

I couldn't believe my luck!

---

A little while after we moved into our new house, Rhys took me out to dinner. We had a lovely steak dinner and then Rhys gave me a card saying how happy he was we’d bought the house together and how excited he was to spend the rest of our lives together. He’d tucked the card into his back and trousers then pulled it out from under his shirt. Even after over 7 years together Rhys is still one for (in this case, slightly sweaty!) romantic gestures so I wasn’t suspicious.
We walked home and Rhys mentioned he’d put the washing on. When we got in I took it out of the washing machine and, classy lady that I am, ran upstairs to change into my usual house attire. I came out to find Rhys already putting the washing out so I hugged him and asked if he wanted a beer. He flinched (turns out the ring was in his pocket!) and shooed me off to get him one so I got the lounge set up with drinks and a TV show. When Rhys didn’t appear I wandered into the kitchen to find him staring at the cupboards apparently having some sort of stroke. I could hear his heart beating from over a metre away and was anxiously asking if he was ok. It was then I realised what was happening...and the fact I’d completely messed it up. I looked down and realised that the outfit I’d selected for this momentous occasion in our relationship wasn’t the lovely dress and heels I’d worn to dinner, but a hoodie, sweat pants and fluffy socks!
Despite my innocent sabotage of his carefully-prepared proposal, Rhys manfully powered through and said a beautiful speech while I had my own mini meltdown - scared for his heart health, in disbelief, part crying and part laughing. With all this going on it turns out I forgot to actually answer the question, and had assumed Rhys knew ‘yes’ was a given. When prompted I did manage to respond appropriately (in my view at least) but, as I’m now regularly reminded, apparently ‘oh, obviously yes!’ isn’t the correct way to respond!
